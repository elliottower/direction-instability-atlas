Dear Editors,

I am submitting **"A Pre-Registered Direction Instability Atlas Across Three Perturbation Modalities"** for consideration as a Data Note in *GigaScience*.

The paper presents a direction instability (DI) atlas spanning LINCS L1000 transcriptomics, Tahoe-100M single-cell transcriptomics, and JUMP Cell Painting morphology (CRISPR knockouts and ORF overexpressions), providing magnitude-corrected DI with bootstrap confidence intervals for each perturbation. DI and its validity are established in a companion paper, deposited at Zenodo (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.21213700); this study characterizes what DI captures across modalities and perturbation types.

Eight primary hypotheses and one exploratory analysis were pre-registered across two batches, with commit SHAs frozen before DI was computed on the new datasets. DI is concordant across expression and morphology modalities (partial rho = 0.19, n = 378). It is not reducible to simpler gene-level features: essential genes show lower DI, knockout and overexpression DI are uncorrelated, and expression variance, paralog count, and paralog expression breadth each explain under 0.4% of variance after confound control. All results are reported against the frozen criteria, including those that failed.

The atlas, pre-registration documents, and analysis code are deposited on Zenodo (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.21223668). All source data derive from public datasets cited in the manuscript. I am the sole author and declare no competing interests. The manuscript is not under consideration elsewhere.
